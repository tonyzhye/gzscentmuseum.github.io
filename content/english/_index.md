    content: |
      Guangzhou Perfume & Spice Museum is nestled among the century-old European-style buildings on Shamian Island. The museum houses 3 exhibition zones, showcasing over 300 pieces of rare essential oils and spices, as well as a collection of more than 1,000 Chinese and foreign exhibits. These exhibits include ancient bronze distillers, Chinese and foreign aromatherapy utensils, Baccarat crystal perfume bottles from Europe’s Golden Age, renowned historical perfume brands along with their limited and classic editions, and historical documents, books, periodicals and papers related to perfumes and spices. Together, they weave a grand tapestry tracing the millennia-long history of fragrance utilization in human civilization.

      
  - title: "Fragrance & Spice History Gallery"
    backgroundColor: "rgba(233, 226, 216, 1)"
    image: "/images//home/feature-1.png"
    content: |
      Located at the core staircase of the museum, this exhibition takes advantage of the vertical depth of the stairwell as its display carrier. Through the linear unfolding of a chronological timeline, it connects 18 pivotal historical milestones in the evolution of fragrances and spices — ranging from the first use of spices in ancient civilizations to the technological innovations of the modern perfume industry. As visitors ascend the stairs step by step, they will feel as if traveling through time and space, experiencing an immersive quick tour of the entire history of fragrances and spices.

      This zone brings together over 300 pieces of rare essential oil spices, covering both classic varieties such as Damascus rose and Australian sandalwood, as well as scarce spice specimens. Here, aromatic utensils from different eras and elegant, understated incense burners are arranged in an exquisite order. Vintage copper distillation pots showcase the traditional craftsmanship of fragrance and spice production. There are also a large collection of books on fragrances and spices, creating a magnificent feast of sight and aroma for every visitor.
